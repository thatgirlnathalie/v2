Easy Filter Onboarding

UX DESIGN | RESEARCH | PROTOTYPING

Experience a fast and easy way to shop.

BFL Group is one of the world’s leading off-price retailers of fashion that is based in the United Arab Emirates and serves over three markets across the Middle East and Europe. Known for its unique “Treasure Hunt” model, Brands for Less ensures that there is always something new to explore, desire, and discover.

ROLE
DURATION
UX DESIGNER
NOV 2020 - FEB 2021

Overview

Concurrent with the launch of the new BFL website and the surge in our online platform, consumers increasingly “went digital” as lockdowns became the new normal due to the global pandemic. Few months after launching the new website and BFL app, reveals that our shoppers are struggling with some usability issues.

The Challenge

Despite sharing the same goal of finding products, shoppers have different needs and intentions. As with most cases, speed is an important factor. This is where product filtering comes into play. The time it takes for the products to load after a new filter is applied is crucial. It may discourage visitors from using the filters or worse — increases page abandonment.

These challenges brought usability questions:

  1. How can we help users leverage the power of filters when searching for attribute values
  2. How can we reduce the number of clicks the shoppers have to make in order for them to apply a new filter?
  3. How can we encourage them to start using the filters again?

Goals and Metrics

Filters simplify the task by narrowing the displayed results to reflect items as per the user’s need and not the entire website of ‘related’ products. The requirement was to implement a fast and easy shopping experience for BFL shoppers, and our goal is to improve the user’s product filtering experience that allows them to apply multiple filter values and increase engagement and retention of shoppers.

The Process

Determined to improve the shoppers' filtering experience, we conducted six rounds of user testing. We analyzed our findings and defined iterations based on feedback to better understand shoppers' pain points. It was then translated into hi-fi prototypes.

Research

User Research & Key Findings

To better understand and address our shoppers' pain points, I interviewed a few friends about how they commonly shop on our mobile app.

User Persona

Using the key findings as a starting point, I developed a provisional user persona that can be used to communicate information gathered during the design process.

Ideation

Concept & Strategy

Focusing on how shoppers browse, filter, and evaluate products on search results and category pages, we decided to provide them with an onboarding experience that will be visible on the app or website's homepage — allowing them to quickly select their desired filters through a modal window.

User Flows

To effectively communicate the concept and information architecture, I created user flows and worked closely with the development team to establish product frameworks.

Lo-Fi Sketches

I sketched multiple options for each screen, referencing the target audience, company mission, and objectives throughout the process to create a successful onboarding experience.

Prototype

Hi-Fi Prototype

The in-app experience begins with brief onboarding questions. Now, shoppers can choose what they're looking for, the styles they like, and the sizes they usually wear in just a few taps.

Responsive Design

The contents of the Easy Filter Onboarding modal window differs greatly for each breakpoint but offers the same functionality and features in each.

Conclusion

The feedback we received from shoppers was extremely positive, with many noting the convenience of being able to navigate and apply their desired filter easily.

Below is the results from our Hi-Fi validation:

  • Reduced time cycle of having to navigate to their desired filter by implementing an onboarding experience with modal window
  • Improved users' experience of finding their category of choice
  • Improved product discoverability and users' interactions with the products, thus, increase in retention and engagement from shoppers
